Financial Stewardship

Money is a powerful tool that can either serve God’s kingdom or become a snare that distracts us from Him. The Bible calls us to be faithful stewards of all God has entrusted to us, including our finances.

“Moreover it is required in stewards, that a man be found faithful” (1 Corinthians 4:2).
“Our temporal possessions are entrusted to us by God, to be used to advance His cause” (Counsels on Stewardship, p. 27).

How we manage money reveals the true condition of our hearts.

The foundation of Christian stewardship is recognizing that everything belongs to God.

“The earth is the Lord’s, and the fullness thereof” (Psalm 24:1).

We are merely caretakers of His resources. This mindset frees us from greed and encourages generosity.

“The Lord calls for men and women who will consecrate their means to His service” (Counsels on Stewardship, p. 39).

Viewing money as God’s property transforms how we earn, save, and spend.

While money itself is neutral, the Bible warns against loving it:

“For the love of money is the root of all evil” (1 Timothy 6:10).

It can easily become an idol, leading to anxiety, selfishness, and spiritual ruin.

“Money is a talent that God has entrusted to us to improve for His glory, not to hoard for selfish purposes” (Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 6, p. 367).

The Christian’s heart must be free from greed, trusting God’s provision instead.

A vital part of stewardship is generous giving.

“God loveth a cheerful giver” (2 Corinthians 9:7).

Ellen White encouraged believers to:

“Pay their tithes and offerings promptly and liberally, that the work of God may advance” (Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 3, p. 383).

Giving is an act of worship and trust—an investment in eternal treasures, not just earthly wealth.

Stewardship also involves wise budgeting, avoiding debt, and living within our means. The Bible advises prudence:

“Go to the ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise” (Proverbs 6:6).
“Godliness with contentment is great gain” (1 Timothy 6:6).

Ellen White taught that those who are faithful in small things, money included, are entrusted with greater responsibilities.

“He who is faithful in that which is least is faithful also in much” (Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 4, p. 176).

Financial stewardship is a sacred responsibility that reflects our faith and priorities. By recognizing God’s ownership, avoiding the love of money, practicing generosity, and managing wisely, we honor Him with our resources.

“Faithful stewardship is a mark of true discipleship” (Counsels on Stewardship, p. 40).

Let us use our finances to advance God’s kingdom and reflect His character in all we do.
