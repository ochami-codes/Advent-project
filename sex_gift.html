Sex: A Gift From God

Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness, neither shadow of turning. — James 1:17 KJV

As with every gift from God, sex is pure, clean, and holy. Yet, in many cultures, merely mentioning the word sex raises eyebrows. Parents struggle to teach their children about it, and society often treats it as a taboo topic. The result is a generation that perceives sexual intimacy as mere pleasure and seeks it indiscriminately.

God is the Author of sex, and to understand it correctly, we must study both the Holy Scriptures and the Spirit of Prophecy. Only then can we grasp the profound spiritual, emotional, and physical dimensions of this gift.

Sex Was Meant for Marriage

Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh. — Genesis 2:24 KJV

God designed sex to be a blessing within marriage—a sacred act that unites husband and wife in emotional, spiritual, and physical oneness. When used according to His will, it strengthens relationships and reflects God’s design for intimacy.

Sexual Excesses Are Against God's Will

The marriage covenant covers sins of the darkest hue. Men and women professing godliness debase their own bodies through the indulgence of corrupt passions, and thus lower themselves beneath the brute creation. They abuse the powers that God has given them to be preserved in sanctification and honor. Health and life are sacrificed upon the altar of base passion. — TSB 110.4

Marriage does not give license for sexual excess. Overindulgence can be physically and spiritually harmful. Nutritionally, excessive sexual activity may lead to the loss of vital elements such as zinc, which is essential for immunity, hormone balance, and overall health. Ellen White further emphasizes:

Sexual excess will effectually destroy a love for devotional exercises, will take from the brain the substance needed to nourish the system, and will most effectually exhaust the vitality. — TSB 113.1

She also warns:

Many professed Christians are suffering with paralysis of nerve and brain because of their intemperance in this direction. Rottenness is in the bones and marrow of many who are regarded as good men, who pray and weep and who stand in high places, but whose polluted carcasses will never pass the portals of the heavenly city. — TSB 113.2

Scientifically, research confirms that sexual overindulgence can lead to physical exhaustion, weakened immunity, and neurological fatigue. One study in the Journal of Sexual Medicine notes that excessive sexual activity may deplete essential neurotransmitters, reduce energy levels, and negatively affect mental focus. This aligns closely with the warnings given in the Spirit of Prophecy.

Conclusion

Sex is a divine gift designed to unite married couples in love, intimacy, and oneness. When misused, it can lead to spiritual, emotional, and physical harm. By understanding its sacred purpose through Scripture, Spirit of Prophecy guidance, and scientific knowledge, we can honor God, protect our health, and enjoy the blessings intended within the covenant of marriage.
