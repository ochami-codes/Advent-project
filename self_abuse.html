Masturbation: Does God Approve?

By definition, masturbation is the stimulation of one’s sexual organs with the aim of attaining sexual pleasure. The world views it as a normal part of human life and even claims it is “necessary.” But does the God of the Bible sanction this?

God’s Original Plan for Sex and Intimacy

From the beginning, God designed sexuality with a sacred purpose — as the means of uniting man and woman in marriage.

Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh. Genesis 2:24, KJV

Sex was created as the means of bonding between husband and wife — physically, emotionally, and spiritually. Masturbation, however, is a selfish distortion of this plan. Instead of “two becoming one,” it becomes “one with oneself.”

Effects of Masturbation

1. Spiritual Consequences — A Barrier to Heaven

Whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart. Matthew 5:28, KJV

Self-abuse is sustained by sexual fantasies and impure imaginations. It enslaves the mind, making the soul unfit for heaven unless repented of.

If you destroy the moral sensibilities, you become a willing slave to Satan. Messages to Young People, p. 63

2. Physical Health Effects

Each act of ejaculation causes the body to lose essential nutrients including zinc, selenium, and lecithin, which are vital for brain function, immunity, and fertility.

Zinc deficiency is linked to low testosterone, hair loss, and poor immunity. Selenium plays a key role in sperm production and antioxidant defense; deficiency may cause fatigue and neurological problems.
Self-abuse is destroying the vital forces of the system. Nervous weakness, despondency, and greatly impaired digestion are generally the result. Child Guidance, p. 439

3. Psychological and Emotional Harm

The temporary pleasure is quickly followed by guilt, shame, and low self-esteem. This emotional cycle weakens willpower and confidence, reducing natural desire for one’s spouse and opening the door to perversions.

The practice of secret vice soon disorders all the faculties of the mind. It weakens the memory and enfeebles the intellect. Testimonies, Vol. 2, p. 349

4. Dangers of Insanity and Nervous Disorders

Self-abuse is the cause of untold sufferings to thousands. It is destroying the nervous system, enfeebling the body, and affecting the brain. Some have even become insane. Testimonies, Vol. 2, p. 347
Modern psychiatry shows that pornography-driven masturbation alters brain reward circuits, leading to addiction, anxiety, depression, and in severe cases mental instability.

Hope for the Struggling Soul

If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. 1 John 1:9, KJV

Victory comes through Christ — by surrender, prayer, guarding the mind, filling the life with active service, and surrounding oneself with holy influences.

Christ is ready to set us free from the slavery of sin, but He does not force the will. If we are willing to be made free, He will set us free indeed. Steps to Christ, p. 47

Conclusion

Masturbation is neither “normal” nor “necessary.” It perverts God’s design, damages health, robs the soul of peace, and endangers mental stability. But through Christ, there is forgiveness and transforming power.

Having therefore these promises, dearly beloved, let us cleanse ourselves from all filthiness of the flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness in the fear of God. 2 Corinthians 7:1, KJV
