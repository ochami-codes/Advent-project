Raising Godly Children

Guiding the Next Generation for the Kingdom of God

Introduction

Raising children in today’s world is a great responsibility and a sacred trust. As Christian parents and guardians, we are called to raise godly offspring who know, love, and obey the Lord. This article explores principles from the Bible and the Spirit of Prophecy on nurturing children for the glory of God.

1. Children Are a Heritage from the Lord

The Bible reminds us that children are not merely ours—they are God's gift and should be raised with reverence and purpose.

"Lo, children are an heritage of the Lord: and the fruit of the womb is his reward." — Psalm 127:3
"Children are the Lord’s heritage, and we are answerable to Him for our management of His property." — Child Guidance, p. 43

2. Early Training Shapes the Future

The early years are crucial. Teaching obedience, reverence, and love for God in childhood lays a strong spiritual foundation.

"Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it." — Proverbs 22:6
"The lessons that the child learns during the first seven years of life have more to do with forming his character than all that it learns in future years." — Child Guidance, p. 193

3. Home as the First School

Parents are the first teachers. A godly home environment, filled with prayer and the Word of God, is the best school for character development.

"The home should be to the children the most attractive place in the world, and the mother’s presence should be its greatest charm." — Adventist Home, p. 21
"And these words... thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children... and shalt talk of them when thou sittest in thine house." — Deuteronomy 6:6-7

4. Teaching by Example

Children learn more by what they see than what they are told. A parent’s own walk with God is a powerful influence.

"By their own example, parents are to show the value of self-control, obedience, and reverence." — Child Guidance, p. 216
"Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven." — Matthew 5:16

Conclusion

Raising godly children is a high calling that requires prayer, patience, and the guidance of God’s Word. With Christ as the center of the home, parents can raise a generation that will stand faithful in a world of compromise. Let us press forward, relying on God's strength, as we train our children for heaven.
