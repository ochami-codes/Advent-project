The Bible on Budgeting

Faithful Planning for Godly Living

Introduction

Budgeting is more than a financial practice—it's a spiritual discipline. While the Bible doesn’t use the term “budget,” it teaches principles of stewardship, planning, and faithfulness that apply directly to managing our finances for God's glory.

1. God Owns Everything

Recognizing God's ownership is the first step to biblical budgeting. We are not owners, but stewards.

"The earth is the Lord’s, and the fullness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein." — Psalm 24:1

2. Count the Cost

Budgeting is about planning and foresight. Jesus illustrated this principle in a practical way:

"For which of you, intending to build a tower, sitteth not down first, and counteth the cost, whether he have sufficient to finish it?" — Luke 14:28

3. Avoid Debt and Save for Lean Times

The Bible warns about the dangers of debt and promotes wise preparation.

"The borrower is servant to the lender." — Proverbs 22:7
"And that food shall be for store to the land against the seven years of famine." — Genesis 41:36

4. Faithful Stewardship Is a Spiritual Duty

Good stewardship honors God and blesses others. Ellen G. White wrote:

"All should learn how to keep accounts. Some neglect this work as nonessential; but this is wrong. All expenses should be accurately stated." — Counsels on Stewardship, p. 294
"Money is not ours; houses and grounds, pictures and furniture, are not ours. They are the Lord’s, and should be used to glorify His name." — Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 5, p. 154

Conclusion

Budgeting is a powerful tool for spiritual growth and financial freedom. It reflects our loyalty to God, enables us to avoid unnecessary debt, and helps us prioritize heavenly investments. Let us take seriously the call to manage God's resources wisely, with diligence and prayer.
